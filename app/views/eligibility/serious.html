The teacher could be stopped from teaching for life if they're found guilty of serious misconduct. Examples of serious misconduct are:

  • violent behaviour
  • a serious criminal offence
  • an abuse of position or trust
  • inappropriate sexual activity
  • a serious failure in safeguarding
  • a sexual or romantic relationship with a current or former pupil

If you refer the teacher for something which is not serious misconduct, it will not be investigated. You should make a complaint instead.

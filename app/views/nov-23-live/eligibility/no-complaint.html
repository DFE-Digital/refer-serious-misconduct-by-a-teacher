Follow these steps to make a complaint:

1. Contact the school to make an informal complaint

You can make an informal complaint. The school will tell you when they will respond.

After the school reviews your complaint, you’ll be told the outcome.

2. Contact the school to make a formal complaint

If the problem is not resolved, follow the school’s complaints process to make a formal complaint.

Every school in England must have a complaints policy. It’s usually available on the school’s website or from the school office. It should tell you the kind of complaints the school deals with.

3. Contact the board of governors or your local council

If you’re not satisfied with how the school has handled your complaint, contact the board of governors or your local council.

4. Make a complaint to the Department for Education (DfE)

If you’re still not satisfied with how the school dealt with your complaint, you can make a complaint to the DfE.

DfE will only consider your complaint if either of the following apply:

  • you have completed the school’s complaints process
  • you have been stopped from completing the school’s complaints process

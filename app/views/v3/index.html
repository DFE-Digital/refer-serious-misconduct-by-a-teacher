Report serious teacher misconduct

{{ govukWarningText({ html: 'Contact the police if you think a child is in immediate danger.' }) }}

Before you report a teacher, you should complain to:

  • the school or sixth form college
  • the board of governors
  • your local council
The Teaching Regulation Agency (TRA) only investigates misconduct if it's serious enough for the teacher to be prevented from teaching again.

Examples of serious misconduct include:

  • sexual misconduct
  • violent behaviour
  • serious failure to protect the safety and wellbeing of pupils
  • alcohol or drug misuse
  • fraud or serious dishonesty
  • discrimination or harassment
  • promoting extreme political or religious views
  • serious criminal offences

Before you start

You’ll need to provide:

  • your contact details and information about the teacher you are reporting
  • evidence to support your allegation, for example signed witness statements or previous complaints against the teacher

You should allow an hour or more to complete your referral. The time it will take depends on the case. It will be faster if you’ve prepared the evidence.

Once you've signed in, you'll be able to save your answers and come back later to complete the referral.

Other ways to report serious teacher misconduct

By email

Fill in the teacher misconduct referral form for employers or the teacher misconduct referral form for members of the public.

Email the form to the TRA.

misconduct.teacher@education.gov.uk

By post

Send the form by registered post.

5 Quinton Road
Coventry
CV1 2WT

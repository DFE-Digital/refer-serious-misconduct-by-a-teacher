What counts as serious misconduct

Serious misconduct by a teacher is any behaviour that:

  • falls significantly short of what is expected of them
  • could damage people's view of the teaching profession
  • led to the conviction of a criminal offence relevant to their fitness to teach

TRA treats each case on its own merits, but serious misconduct can include:

  • sexual misconduct
  • violent behaviour
  • serious failure to protect the safety and wellbeing of pupils
  • alcohol or drug misuse
  • fraud or serious dishonesty
  • discrimination or harassment
  • promoting extreme political or religious views

TRA will not investigate cases involving incompetence or underperformance. You should complain to the school instead.
